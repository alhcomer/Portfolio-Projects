My Timeline

2015 - 2018

BSc Marine Biology - University of Liverpool

Having had a strong interest in natural sciences and sustainability, pursuing an undergraduate degree in Marine Biology was a natural fit that allowed me to develop my understanding of the environment and our human place within that. Alongside this, I honed my ability to to think in a scientific, considerate and critical manner while also learning how to effectively collect, analyse, interpret and present data.

2018 - 2019

MSc Environment and Development - University of Leeds

To develop a more comprehensive and holistic understanding of sustainability with a stronger focus on social aspects, I pursued a Masters In Environment & Development. This gave me a greater appreciation for how sustainability relates to our political and economic landscape. Studying at this higher level greatly improved my ability to adapt quickly. Many modules studied required a significant prerequisite understanding that, because I had not studied these areas previously, meant I had to teach myself large amounts of content from a number of subject areas, such as economics, business and project management.

2019 - 2022

Waiter - Côte Brasserie, Manchester

While waiting tables was entirely unrelated to my prior studies, I am thankful for this time as it is when I developed my interest in programming. From struggling to write a blackjack program to making my first full-stack web application, this time was valuable for me in learning key concepts and developing my ability to ‘think like a programmer’.

2022 - Present

Chef de Partie - Côte Brasserie, Manchester

Moving into the kitchen at Côte has allowed me to indulge my other passion – cooking. While in this role I not only developed my culinary skills, but also my abilities to stay organised, work as a team and deal with intense pressure. In this time I have maintained my independent programming studies and pursued a greater depth of understanding to include other languages and computer science theory.
